The One Percent Company

The problem: Capitalism

Here's a thought.

The whole premise of companies and new-age startups relies on possibilities of getting funded by financial institutions and individuals at any or many stages of their growth and development.

This has been an age-long practice and applies in many fields of work, including and not limited to engineering, medicine, technology, etc. The model has proven to be successful over the years, and has given us great businesses and marvelous institutions.

Great business models also build great billionaires and millionaires. And rightly so, because they deserve it owing to all their hard work and perseverence. These enterpreneurs and venture capitalists shape the world with their far-fetched vision, unique observability and ability to solve problems at scale.

Here's the problem though.

Great businesses are build with great teams. And teams put their sweat and time into bringing the founders' vision to fruition. But they aren't paid as much as the people at the top, the shareholders, who hold the majority shares in the company. Many a times, these people at the top are not even the founders, having gone through multiple rounds of funding.

The owners of businesses sometimes sell away parts of their company to people with money to bootstrap, or achieve growth at rocket speed. And the people with money become people with more money, while the teams whose hard work brings in the money, are disposed to salaries that are minute compared to the shareholders.

Businesses in the present are means to solving problems and making money to fill in cash vaults. The rich have found ways to make their money while they sip away fancy beachside cocktails on vacations, and having the investees work hard for their cash vaults.

Although this isn't the case with many well organised businesses, but is definitely the case with many others.

The current state of employment is that the employees are paid for the time put into work, instead of the impact created by their work. The time rate of each employee is pre-determined during interviews and the outcome is the weekly/monthly salary that stays the same and increases gradually with experience. At best, there are employee stock option pools which is a very small sliver of the cake as compared to the investors.

Disclaimer:
The above problem is a little argumentative. For many, it may not even be a problem. If you don't see this as a problem, you're free to leave this website at once, because the rest of content is not for you.

The Solution: Anti Capitalism

It's simple. The team deserves the profits that the company makes.

And the team comprises of everyone.
Founders, developers, investors, designers, sales, operations, customer support. Every ONE!

The question is how is it to be achieved. Although the solution is plain simple, it's implementation can become quite tricky. In an ideal world, every individual should be monetarily compensated for the impact he/she creates in creating company's profits. This can be achieved if there was an way to easily calculate everyone's impact on the organisation's profits. There are ways to do it, and this is what this company's organisational hirerchy is about.

Feel free to join the slack community to find more about how we are tackling this problem, and if you want to play a part in bringing about this change.

Also, a problem might not be a problem for everyone. This is completely subjective. Please join the discussion if you feel that this is a problem that needs to be solved.
